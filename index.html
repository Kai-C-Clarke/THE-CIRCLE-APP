Guy to henry@askian.net
Is alcohol dangerous?
Henry VIII replied
By God's blood, this is a strange and sudden question to put before a king. I shall answer it plainly: all things God hath made may be used well or ill. Wine and ale are gifts to gladden the heart of man, as Scripture saith. In moderation, they are wholesome; in excess, they lead to folly, vice, and sickness of the body.

I myself take a cup of good Gascon wine with my meals, and it pleaseth me well. But I have seen men made fools of themselves by drinking to surfeit.

If you cannot hold your drink, you had best hold your peace and avoid it. A clear head serves a man better than a fuddled one, in matters of state, faith, or honour.
Henry R
